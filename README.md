# Digital Ops Con Ed Refresh

Production-oriented digital operations, messaging, and UX refresh case study for a school-focused IT support provider.

## Overview

Digital Ops Con Ed Refresh documents a completed digital strategy and website messaging project for Con Ed, an IT support provider serving primary schools across Hertfordshire. The repository captures the strategic repositioning, homepage structure, visual concepts, and supporting campaign practice assets created during the delivery sprint.

The project is not an application codebase. It is a production-facing digital operations and content strategy repository containing documentation, visual assets, and campaign frameworks.

## The Operational Problem

Con Ed's service model is operationally valuable: relationship-led, weekly on-site technical support for schools. The problem was that the previous digital presence did not clearly communicate that value to the people responsible for buying school IT support.

The operational challenges included:

- School leaders needed fast clarity on what support they would receive and why it mattered.
- Technical service lists risked obscuring the strongest differentiator: consistent weekly on-site technician presence.
- Non-technical buyers such as headteachers, bursars, and school business managers needed plain-English outcomes, not infrastructure jargon.
- The website needed to support trust, local relevance, and inquiry generation.
- The digital workflow needed assets that could be reviewed, iterated, and delivered professionally.

## The Solution

The refresh centralizes Con Ed's positioning around practical school outcomes: reliable support, friendly on-site presence, fewer classroom disruptions, and trusted local service. Instead of leading with technical inventory, the strategy frames IT support through operational continuity for primary schools.

The repository includes:

- Homepage messaging strategy.
- UX structure for a refreshed landing experience.
- Visual concept assets for hero, services, trust, and call-to-action sections.
- Local SEO positioning around primary school IT support in Hertfordshire.
- Bonus campaign frameworks for adjacent B2B technical communication practice.

## Production-Oriented Development Lifecycle

This project reflects a practical production lifecycle for digital operations work:

1. **Discovery:** Identify the buyer, decision context, and real operational pain.
2. **Positioning:** Shift from generic IT service lists to the strongest business differentiator.
3. **Information architecture:** Structure the homepage around trust, problem recognition, service clarity, and conversion.
4. **Copywriting:** Translate technical delivery into school-friendly language.
5. **Visual concepting:** Produce concrete section mockups for stakeholder review.
6. **SEO planning:** Align copy with local, high-intent search language.
7. **Delivery:** Package the recommendations, assets, and supporting rationale for handoff.

## Key Deliverables

- **Digital positioning strategy:** Reframes Con Ed as a relationship-led school IT partner.
- **Homepage refresh direction:** Defines the hero message, service grouping, trust factors, and call-to-action flow.
- **UX mockup assets:** Includes visual concepts stored in `assets/`.

- **Audience-aligned copy:** Uses plain English for non-technical school decision-makers.
