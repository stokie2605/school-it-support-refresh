### 1. Objective
[cite_start]Design a practical lead magnet campaign that helps engineering and technical businesses understand whether they are ready for Cyber Essentials, while generating qualified enquiries for ac&e's Cyber Essentials advisory service[cite: 270]. ### 2. Brief Research
[cite_start]Cyber Essentials is a UK Government-backed cyber security certification scheme[cite: 273]. [cite_start]The NCSC describes it as a minimum cyber security standard for organisations of all sizes, based around five technical controls: firewalls, secure configuration, user access control, malware protection, and security update management[cite: 274, 275, 276, 277, 278, 279].

* [cite_start]**Delivery Partner:** IASME is the NCSC's official Cyber Essentials delivery partner and provides preparation resources[cite: 280].
* [cite_start]**Competitor Positioning:** Competitor and advisory pages often position support around making certification simpler, readiness assessments, gap analysis, and remediation support[cite: 281].
* [cite_start]**The Engineering Focus:** The opportunity for ac&e is to make the lead magnet specific to engineering businesses, rather than creating another generic SME cyber checklist[cite: 282].

---

### 3. Target Audience & Needs
[cite_start]The main audience is engineering and technical companies that may need Cyber Essentials to win public sector, defence, supply chain or larger client contracts[cite: 295]. [cite_start]They are likely to use CAD workstations, simulation tools, HPC infrastructure, specialist engineering software, and shared project files[cite: 297, 298, 299, 300, 301]. [cite_start]They are busy, technical, and skeptical of generic marketing[cite: 305].

### 4. Recommended Lead Magnet: Readiness Checklist
[cite_start]A short downloadable checklist that helps engineering and technical businesses spot common Cyber Essentials gaps before they begin certification[cite: 291, 292].

* **Key Checklist Verification Points:**
  * [cite_start]Have you listed the devices, servers, cloud services and workstations that are in scope[cite: 336]?
  * [cite_start]Are CAD, simulation and engineering workstations included in your review[cite: 337]?
  * [cite_start]Is multi-factor authentication enabled on cloud services[cite: 339]?
  * [cite_start]Are operating systems, engineering tools and business software updated regularly[cite: 340]?
  * [cite_start]Is malware protection active on laptops, desktops and servers[cite: 344]?
