# ac&e Cyber Essentials Lead Magnet Campaign
## Bonus Concept Note

### 1. Objective
Design a practical lead magnet campaign that helps engineering and technical businesses understand whether they are ready for Cyber Essentials, while generating qualified enquiries for ac&e's Cyber Essentials advisory service. The focus is strategy, recommendations and prototype content, not building a live landing page.

---

### 2. Brief Research
Cyber Essentials is a UK Government-backed cyber security certification scheme. The NCSC describes it as a minimum cyber security standard for organisations of all sizes, based around five technical controls: firewalls, secure configuration, user access control, malware protection, and security update management.

* **Delivery Partner:** IASME is the NCSC's official Cyber Essentials delivery partner and provides preparation resources.
* **Competitor Positioning:** Competitor and advisory pages often position support around making certification simpler, readiness assessments, gap analysis, and remediation support.
* **The Engineering Focus:** The opportunity for ac&e is to make the lead magnet specific to engineering businesses, rather than creating another generic SME cyber checklist.

---

### 3. Target Audience & Needs
The main audience is engineering and technical companies that may need Cyber Essentials to win public sector, defence, supply chain or larger client contracts. They are likely to use CAD workstations, simulation tools, HPC infrastructure, specialist engineering software, and shared project files. They are busy, technical, and skeptical of generic marketing.

---

### 4. Recommended Lead Magnet: Readiness Checklist
A short downloadable checklist that helps engineering and technical businesses spot common Cyber Essentials gaps before they begin certification.

* **Key Checklist Verification Points:**
  * Have you listed the devices, servers, cloud services and workstations that are in scope?
  * Are CAD, simulation and engineering workstations included in your review?
  * Is multi-factor authentication enabled on cloud services?
  * Are operating systems, engineering tools and business software updated regularly?
  * Is malware protection active on laptops, desktops and servers?

---

### 5. Draft Landing Page Copy
* **Headline:** Could your engineering business pass Cyber Essentials?
* **Supporting Copy:** Many engineering businesses need Cyber Essentials to win public sector, defence or supply chain contracts, but the process can feel unclear and time-consuming. This free checklist helps you review the main areas before you start: devices, users, updates, access, firewalls, malware protection and cloud services.
* **Call to Action:** Download the readiness checklist.

---

### 6. Follow-Up Email Sequence
* **Email 1 (Checklist Delivery):** Delivers the checklist. Notes that if gaps are identified, ac&e can help review their position before certification begins.
* **Email 2 (Common Gaps):** Discusses complex engineering environments (specialist software, remote access, HPC) and common gaps like unclear device scope or missing cloud MFA.
* **Email 3 (Readiness Call):** Offers a practical readiness call to check likely gaps before formal submission.
