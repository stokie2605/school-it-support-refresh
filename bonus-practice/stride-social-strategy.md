### 1. Objective
[cite_start]Design a practical social media strategy for STRIDE Simulation Technology that helps build technical credibility, attract inbound interest, and support recruitment by sharing useful simulation knowledge with engineers[cite: 401].

### 2. Positioning & Recommended Platform
* [cite_start]**Primary Platform: YouTube** [cite: 424]
* [cite_start]**Why it works:** Engineering simulation topics naturally need explanation, screen visuals, examples, and demonstrations[cite: 425]. [cite_start]While LinkedIn is great for sharing, YouTube acts as a searchable technical knowledge base where videos keep gaining value over time[cite: 425, 433].
* [cite_start]**Channel Concept:** *Practical Simulation Notes from STRIDE*[cite: 436]. [cite_start]It shouldn't feel like polished influencer content; it should feel like experienced engineers explaining useful simulation ideas clearly without marketing hype[cite: 437, 441].

[cite_start]Simulation engineers, hydraulic modellers, CFD/FEA engineers, engineering managers, and technical leads in water, automotive, aerospace, and industrial sectors[cite: 444, 445, 446, 447, 448, 449]. ### 5. Production Approach for a Small Team
1. [cite_start]Pick one narrow engineering question[cite: 480].
2. [cite_start]Capture rough bullet points or a short voice note from a technical team member[cite: 481].
3. [cite_start]Use AI to structure a video outline, then have the expert edit it to ensure tight technical accuracy[cite: 482, 483].
4. [cite_start]Record a simple screen-share or slide walkthrough[cite: 484].
