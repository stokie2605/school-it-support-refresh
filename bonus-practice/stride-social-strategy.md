# STRIDE Simulation Technology Social Media & YouTube Strategy
## Bonus Concept Note

### 1. Objective
Design a practical social media strategy for STRIDE Simulation Technology that helps build technical credibility, attract inbound interest, and support recruitment by sharing useful simulation knowledge with engineers.

---

### 2. Positioning & Recommended Platform
* **Primary Platform: YouTube**
* **Why it works:** Engineering simulation topics naturally need explanation, screen visuals, examples, and demonstrations. While LinkedIn is great for sharing, YouTube acts as a searchable technical knowledge base where videos keep gaining value over time.
* **Channel Concept:** *Practical Simulation Notes from STRIDE*. It shouldn't feel like polished influencer content; it should feel like experienced engineers explaining useful simulation ideas clearly without marketing hype.

---

### 3. Target Audience
Simulation engineers, hydraulic modellers, CFD/FEA engineers, engineering managers, and technical leads in water, automotive, aerospace, and industrial sectors. They respond best to specific examples, practical workflows, and clear engineering reasoning.

---

### 4. Content Pillars & Example Topics
* **Pillar 1: Faster Simulation Workflows** – Focused on reducing manual setups, repeated model changes, and slow verification.
  * *Topic:* Why manual model verification slows down water projects.
* **Pillar 2: Applied Particle-Based CFD** – Showing where fluid simulation solves moving boundary or splashing problems.
  * *Topic:* What makes gearbox lubrication difficult to simulate?
* **Pillar 3: Water and Environmental Modelling** – Targeting wastewater, drainage, flood risk, and infrastructure planning.
  * *Topic:* How automation can support wastewater project delivery.
* **Pillar 4: Engineering Decision Confidence** – Using simulation to make better design decisions earlier to reduce late-stage changes.

---

### 5. Production Approach for a Small Team
1. Pick one narrow engineering question.
2. Capture rough bullet points or a short voice note from a technical team member.
3. Use AI to structure a video outline, then have the expert edit it to ensure tight technical accuracy.
4. Record a simple screen-share or slide walkthrough.
5. Chop the recording into one YouTube video, a text-based LinkedIn post, two short clips, and a short written summary for the website.
